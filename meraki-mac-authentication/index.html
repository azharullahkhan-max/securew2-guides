Cisco Meraki MAC Authentication

Configure MAC-based network access control using SecureW2's JoinNow Management Portal and Cisco Meraki. This guide walks through creating an Identity Provider, setting up Role and Network Policies, and configuring your Meraki SSID to authenticate devices by MAC address.

SecureW2 JoinNow Cisco Meraki MAC Authentication 802.1X

! Overview

What MAC Authentication does and when to use it

MAC Authentication allows network access to be granted or denied based on a device's MAC address, without requiring user credentials or client certificates. SecureW2 acts as the RADIUS server, validating the device MAC against an Identity Provider you configure, and Cisco Meraki enforces the policy on the SSID.

ℹ️
Prerequisites An active SecureW2 JoinNow ASO subscription, access to the JoinNow MultiOS Management Portal, and admin access to the Cisco Meraki Dashboard are required before proceeding.
⚠️
MAC addresses must be registered first Devices must be added to the Identity Provider's Conditions tab before they can authenticate. If a device's MAC address is not listed, authentication will be denied.

🔑 Create Identity Provider in SecureW2

JoinNow MultiOS Management Portal — Identity Management

Prerequisites

  • Admin access to the JoinNow MultiOS Management Portal
  • MAC addresses of all devices to be authenticated
  • Active SecureW2 JoinNow ASO subscription
1
Log in to the JoinNow MultiOS Management Portal

Sign in with your administrator credentials.

2
Navigate to Identity Providers

Go to Identity Management > Identity Providers and click Add Identity Provider.

3
Enter a name and description

In the NAME field, enter a name for your Identity Provider. In the Description field, enter a suitable description.

4
Set the type to MAC Authentication

Under the Type drop-down, select MAC Authentication.

5
Save the Identity Provider

Click Save. The page will refresh.

6
Open the Conditions tab

Click on the Conditions tab that now appears.

7
Add a device MAC address

Click Add Device. In the MAC Address field, enter the MAC address of the device you need to authenticate. Click Save.

8
Finalise the Identity Provider

Click Update to save all changes to the Identity Provider.

✅
Repeat for each device Repeat steps 7–8 for every device MAC address that requires network access. All MAC addresses must be present before Meraki is configured.
✅
Confirmation: The Identity Provider appears in the list under Identity Management > Identity Providers with type MAC Authentication and all required MAC addresses listed under its Conditions tab.

📋 Set up Role Policy and Network Policy

JoinNow MultiOS Management Portal — Policy Management

Role Policy Step 1 of 2

1
Log in to the JoinNow MultiOS Management Portal

Sign in with your administrator credentials.

2
Navigate to Role Policies

Go to Policy Management > Role Policies and click Add Role.

3
Enter a name and description

In the Name field, enter a name for your role policy. In the Display Description field, enter a suitable description. Click Save.

4
Open the Conditions tab

The page refreshes and the Conditions tab appears. Click on it.

5
Select the MAC Authentication Identity Provider

From the Identity Provider drop-down, select the Identity Provider you created with the MAC Authentication type.

6
Save the Role Policy

Click Update to save your changes.

ℹ️
Note Devices for MAC Authentication must be added in the Identity Provider before this Role Policy will match them successfully.

Network Policy Step 2 of 2

1
Navigate to Network Policies

Under Policy Management, click Network Policies.

2
Enter a name and description

In the Name field, enter a name for your network policy. In the Display Description field, enter a suitable description. Click Save.

3
Open the Conditions tab

Click the Conditions tab.

4
Set the match criteria

Select Match All or Match Any based on your requirement. Click Add rule.

5
Add an Identity rule

Click Identity, then click Select adjacent to the Role option. Click Save. The Role option now appears under the Conditions tab.

6
Select the Role Policy

From the Role Equals drop-down, select the role policy you created in the previous section.

7
Save the Network Policy

Click Update to save all changes.

✅
Confirmation: Both the Role Policy and Network Policy appear in their respective lists under Policy Management, with the correct Identity Provider and Role references configured.

🔗 Configure Cisco Meraki

Meraki Dashboard — Wireless Access Control & RADIUS

Prerequisites

  • Admin access to the Cisco Meraki Dashboard
  • RADIUS Host IP, Port, and Secret from the SecureW2 Management Portal
  • An existing SSID to configure for MAC-based access control
ℹ️
Where to find RADIUS credentials In the SecureW2 Management Portal, navigate to RADIUS > RADIUS Configuration to retrieve the Host IP, Port, and Secret values needed below.
1
Log in to the Cisco Meraki Portal

Sign in at dashboard.meraki.com with your administrator credentials.

2
Navigate to Access Control

Go to Wireless > Access Control.

3
Select your SSID

From the SSID drop-down, select the SSID you want to configure for MAC-based authentication.

4
Enable MAC-based access control

Under Network Access, select MAC-based access control (No encryption).

5
Set Splash page to None

Under the Splash page setting, select None.

6
Add the SecureW2 RADIUS server

In the RADIUS servers section, click Add a server. Copy and paste the Host IP, Port, and Secret from SecureW2 Management Portal > RADIUS > RADIUS Configuration into the corresponding fields in Meraki.

7
Save the configuration

Scroll to the bottom of the page and click Save changes.

✅
Confirmation: Registered devices connect to the SSID automatically. The Meraki event log (Network-wide > Event log) shows successful RADIUS MAC authentication events with no failures.
